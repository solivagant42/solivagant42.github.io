Archive I – XXXV
Bengaluru
Black & White
2026 – Ongoing

This series began without intention. The route between ICTS and NCBS — two research institutes on opposite ends of Bengaluru — takes roughly forty minutes by bus. I began photographing through the window to pass the time. I have not stopped.

"Each photograph captures a moment. No matter whether it's blurry or in focus, it reveals a depth beneath the surface — layers beneath what was considered ordinary."

Here in these streets, life unfolds in all of its nuances. A vendor mid-gesture. A tree pushing through concrete. A figure caught between one step and the next. These are not exceptional moments — that is precisely the point. The series is a sustained argument that beauty can arise from the mundane and the ordinary.

The blurriness is not failure. Blur is what motion looks like. An out-of-focus image is not a mistake — it is what it means to be moving through a moving city, trying to fix one moment in a stream of moments that have no intention of stopping for you.

The name of the parent blog — Studies of Natural History — is not accidental. These are field notes. The specimen is Bengaluru. The observer is in motion. Black and white removes the distraction of colour. Each archive is one journey. The photographs are not curated — they are a chronology. The discipline is entirely in the shooting.
